In a list (array):
Elements are stored continuously in memory.
So if you remove or insert something in the middle or at the start, all the elements after it must shift one position.
This shifting takes time (O(n)).

In a linked list:
Each element stores only two things: data + address of next.
To insert or delete, you just change a couple of pointers.
No shifting.
This takes constant time (O(1)).
